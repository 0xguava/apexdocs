### 1. What specific problem does ApexaiQ solve?
ApexaiQ solves the problem of "blind spots" in IT infrastructure. It acts as a continuous, agentless Cyber Asset Attack Surface Management (CAASM) platform 
that discovers all devices and software on a network. By consolidating data from existing tools into a single source of truth, it eliminates shadow IT, 
identifies security gaps, and prioritizes vulnerability remediation before risks can be exploited.

### 2. What are the top 5 pain points faced by Enterprises and MSPs?
- **Enterprises:**
    - **Shadow IT:** Untracked devices or unauthorized software introducing hidden risks.
    - **Dirty Data/Inaccurate CMDBs:** Disconnected tools creating conflicting or stale asset records.
    - **Audit Failures:** Struggling to prove compliance due to lack of visibility.
    - **Vulnerability Overload:** Having thousands of alerts but lacking context on what to patch first.
    - **Tool Sprawl:** Paying for overlapping security tools without knowing if they are fully deployed.
- **MSPs (Managed Service Providers):**
    - **Slow Onboarding:** Taking weeks to manually map a new client's environment.
    - **Blind Quoting:** Underpricing contracts because of hidden complexity in a prospect's network.
    - **Revenue Leakage:** Missing out on billing for undiscovered assets or unmanaged devices.
    - **License & Warranty Tracking:** The manual, error-prone process of tracking hardware end-of-life and software true-ups.
    - **Multi-Tenant Complexity:** Struggling to secure and manage multiple disjointed client environments efficiently.

### 3. Cyber Asset
Any digital or physical entity that forms part of an organization's IT environment. This includes hardware (servers, laptops, routers), software, cloud instances, 
IoT devices, and even user identities.

### 4. Asset Discovery
The automated process of scanning networks and integrating with APIs to find, identify, and catalog all IT assets connected to an organization's infrastructure. 

### 5. Shadow IT
Hardware, software, or applications used by employees for business purposes without the explicit approval, knowledge, or oversight of the IT department (e.g., using 
a personal Dropbox account for company files).

### 6. Vulnerabilities
Flaws or weaknesses in an IT system, software code, or hardware design that cybercriminals can exploit to gain unauthorized access, disrupt services, or steal data.

### 7. Standards of Vulns
Standardized frameworks used globally to identify and rate vulnerabilities. 
- **CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures):** A dictionary that provides standardized names for publicly known vulnerabilities.
- **CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System):** A framework for rating the severity of a vulnerability on a scale of 0 to 10.

### 8. What is meant by vuln remediation?
The process of fixing or mitigating a discovered vulnerability. Common remediation steps include applying a software patch, changing a network configuration, or 
decommissioning an outdated device.

### 9. CMDB (Configuration Management Database)
A centralized database used by IT teams to track all hardware and software components (Configuration Items) and map the relationships and dependencies between them. 

### 10. ITSM vs ITAM
- **ITSM (IT Service Management):** Focuses on the *delivery* of IT services to users. Think help desk tickets, incident resolution, and service requests.
- **ITAM (IT Asset Management):** Focuses on the *lifecycle* and costs of the assets themselves. Think tracking laptop warranties, software licenses, and hardware depreciation.

### 11. ApexaiQ Score
This is a proprietary risk metric generated by the ApexaiQ platform. It computes an organization’s overall infrastructure health, obsolescence, and vulnerabilities into 
a single numerical score ranging from 60 to 160 (inspired by the human IQ scale), giving leaders a simplified way to measure and communicate IT risk.

### 12. Attack Surface
The total sum of all potential entry points—known and unknown—where an unauthorized user can try to enter or extract data from an environment. This includes open ports
, unpatched software, and exposed cloud servers.

### 13. MSP and MSSP
- **MSP (Managed Service Provider):** Manages a client's general IT infrastructure, user support, networking, and day-to-day operations.
- **MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider):** Specializes in high-level cybersecurity services, such as 24/7 threat monitoring, incident response, and compliance management.

### 14. Multi-tenancy
An architecture where a single instance of a software application serves multiple distinct customer groups (tenants). For an MSP, this means using one dashboard to 
securely manage 50 different clients without their data mixing.

### 15. SLA Compliance (Service Level Agreement)
The process of meeting the specific performance metrics legally agreed upon between a service provider and a customer (e.g., guaranteeing 99.9% uptime or resolving critical 
tickets within 2 hours).

### 16. RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)
A security framework that restricts system access based on an individual's role within the organization. Employees are only given the minimum permissions necessary to 
perform their jobs.

### 17. Technical Debt
The implied future cost of choosing a fast, easy, or temporary IT workaround today instead of a better, more permanent solution. Over time, "debt" accumulates, 
making systems fragile, hard to update, and expensive to maintain.

### 18. False Positives
When a security tool incorrectly flags a safe action, file, or configuration as a threat or vulnerability. A high rate of false positives wastes analysts' time and 
leads to "alert fatigue."

### 19. CAASM (Cyber Asset Attack Surface Management)
An emerging technology category focusing on securing the attack surface by providing complete visibility into all internal and external assets. It generally relies 
on API integrations with existing IT and security tools to find gaps, rather than using traditional network scanners. 

### 20. EDR and XDR
- **EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response):** Security software installed directly on end-user devices (endpoints like laptops and servers) that monitors behavior to 
detect and stop threats like ransomware.
- **XDR (Extended Detection and Response):** An evolution of EDR that collects and correlates data across the entire IT environment—endpoints, cloud workloads, 
network traffic, and email to provide a faster, more comprehensive response to complex attacks.

### Terms
- **MSP/MSSP**: 
    - Managed Service Providers (MSPs) and Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs)
    both offer outsourced IT services, but with different focuses: MSPs manage general IT infrastructure 
    (networks, help desk), while MSSPs specialize in proactive, 24/7 security monitoring, 
    threat detection, and response.
    - Tenant management involves using a centralized, multi-tenant architecture to manage 
    multiple client environments from a single interface.
- **SLA**: A Service Level Agreement (SLA) is a formal, binding contract defining security-related, 
measurable commitments between a service provider and a client, focusing on metrics like incident response times, uptime,
and data protection, ensuring accountability for security posture. 
- **MTTR**: Mean Time to Repair (MTTR) is the average time required to troubleshoot and fix 
a failed system or component, returning it to full operational status.
- **CMDB**: A configuration management database(CMDB) is a database used by an organization to store information about hardware and software assets.  
- **EDR**: Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) is a cybersecurity solution that continuously 
monitors end-user devices (workstations, servers, laptops) to detect, investigate, and 
mitigate advanced threats like ransomware in real-time.
- **CVE**: CVE stands for Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures. CVE is an archive of vulnerabilities.
- **CVSS**: Common vulnerability scoring system is used to evaluate the threat level of a vulnerability.
It outputs a CVE score.
- **NVD**: The National Vulnerability Database (NVD) is the U.S. government's official repository of public cybersecurity vulnerabilities.
Organizations use it to automate vulnerability management and security compliance.
